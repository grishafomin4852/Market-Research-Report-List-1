<p><p>The specialty yeast market includes three main types: yeast extract, autolytic yeast, and other yeast derivatives. Yeast extract refers to the soluble content obtained after removing the cell walls of yeast cells. Autolytic yeast is created through the self-destruction or rupture of yeast cells, releasing enzymes and nutrients. Other yeast derivatives are derived from yeast and encompass various products like yeast beta-glucan, yeast protein concentrate, and yeast hydrolysate. These specialty yeast types find applications in diverse industries, ranging from food and beverages to pharmaceuticals and animal feed, due to their nutritional value and functional properties.</p></p>
