<p><p>The prebiotic ingredient market encompasses various types, including oligosaccharides, polysaccharides, polyols, and other ingredients. Oligosaccharides are short chains of sugar molecules that promote the growth of beneficial gut bacteria. Polysaccharides, on the other hand, are long chains of sugar molecules that act as prebiotics by providing nourishment to the gut microbiota. Polyols, such as erythritol and xylitol, are sugar alcohols that are not fully digested, thus serving as prebiotic substrates for the gut bacteria. Lastly, the "other" category includes various prebiotic ingredients like resistant starches, dietary fiber, and inulin, which offer similar benefits to gut health.</p></p>
