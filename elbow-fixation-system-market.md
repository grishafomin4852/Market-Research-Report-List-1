<p><p>Elbow fixation systems are orthopedic devices used to treat fractures or injuries in the elbow joint. The market for these systems can be categorized into two types: external fixation and internal fixation. External fixation involves the use of external devices like frames and pins to stabilize and align the fractured bones. Internal fixation, on the other hand, involves the use of implants such as plates, screws, or wires placed internally to stabilize the bones. Both systems aim to provide stability and promote bone healing, but differ in their approach and mode of fixation.</p></p>
