<p><p>Advanced structural ceramics are high-performance materials that are used in demanding applications due to their exceptional mechanical, thermal, and chemical properties. Aluminas, or aluminum oxides, are the most common type of structural ceramics and are known for their high strength and resistance to wear and corrosion. Carbides, such as silicon carbide, exhibit exceptional hardness and heat resistance. Zirconia and zirconia blends offer improved fracture toughness and thermal shock resistance. Nitrides, like silicon nitride, possess excellent mechanical properties and can withstand high temperatures. Other types of advanced structural ceramics include borides, gallium nitride, and titanium diboride.</p></p>
