<p><p>Automotive engine encapsulation refers to a system designed to enhance the fuel efficiency and performance of vehicles by reducing engine noise and improving thermal management. There are two types of encapsulation methods: engine-mounted and body-mounted. Engine-mounted encapsulation involves installing insulation materials directly onto the engine, while body-mounted encapsulation incorporates the insulation materials into the vehicle's body structure. Both methods help to minimize noise and heat transfer from the engine, optimizing the overall driving experience and contributing to improved fuel economy.</p></p>
