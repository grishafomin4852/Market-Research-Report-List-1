<p><p>Speed limiters, also known as speed governors, are electronic devices installed in vehicles to restrict the maximum speed at which they can operate. They are typically used to enhance road safety by preventing drivers from exceeding a certain speed limit. Speed limiters can be implemented through various means, such as reducing the fuel flow to the engine, electronically limiting the throttle, or imposing a maximum rotational speed on the engine.</p><p>The future outlook of the speed limiters market appears promising. With the increasing concern for road safety and the rising number of accidents caused by speeding, governments and regulatory authorities across the world are actively promoting the adoption of speed limiters in vehicles. Many countries have already mandated the use of speed limiters in commercial vehicles, and there is a growing trend towards making them mandatory in all types of vehicles.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in vehicle telematics and connected technologies are expected to drive the demand for speed limiters. Integration of speed limiters with other intelligent systems, such as GPS navigation and driver assistance features, can provide real-time monitoring and alerts to drivers, enabling them to comply with speed limits effectively.</p><p>The speed limiters market is anticipated to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 13.7% during the forecasted period. Moreover, the integration of speed limiters with autonomous driving technologies holds significant potential for market growth.</p></p>
