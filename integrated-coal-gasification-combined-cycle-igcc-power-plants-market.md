<p><p>Integrated Coal Gasification Combined Cycle (IGCC) Power Plants utilize a two-step process to convert coal into gas, which is then used to generate electricity. In the first step, coal is partially oxidized in a high-pressure gasifier, producing a synthesis gas (syngas) consisting of hydrogen and carbon monoxide. The syngas is then cleaned and treated to remove impurities such as sulfur and mercury. In the second step, the clean syngas is utilized in a combined cycle power plant, where it is combusted with air to produce high-pressure steam, which drives a steam turbine. The exhaust gas from the combustion process is then used to generate additional electricity in a gas turbine.</p><p>The Integrated Coal Gasification Combined Cycle (IGCC) Power Plants Market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. <p><p>Integrated Coal Gasification Combined Cycle (IGCC) Power Plants are advanced technologies that convert coal into synthetic gas (syngas) for power generation. There are two types of IGCC plants - Air-blown IGCC and Oxygen-blown IGCC. In Air-blown IGCC, coal is mixed with air and undergoes gasification, while in Oxygen-blown IGCC, coal reacts with pure oxygen. Both processes result in the production of syngas, which further goes through a purification process to remove impurities before being used in gas turbines to generate electricity.</p></p>
