<p><p>The automotive radio market consists of two main types: single DIN and double DIN. Single DIN refers to a smaller sized radio unit that typically measures around 2 inches tall. It fits into standard radio slots and often comes with basic features like FM/AM radio and CD player. On the other hand, double DIN radios are larger, measuring around 4 inches tall, and offer more advanced features like touchscreen displays, Bluetooth connectivity, and navigation systems. These larger units require a double-sized dashboard slot for installation.</p></p>
