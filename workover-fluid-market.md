<p><p>Workover fluid refers to the fluid used during workover operations in oil and gas wells. Two common types of workover fluids are brine and polymer. Brine workover fluids consist of water with high concentrations of salts, such as sodium chloride, and are effective in preventing clay swelling and maintaining wellbore stability. Polymer workover fluids, on the other hand, are composed of synthetic polymers that offer improved lubricity and enhance wellbore cleaning properties. Both types of workover fluids play crucial roles in maintaining and optimizing well performance during workover operations.</p></p>
