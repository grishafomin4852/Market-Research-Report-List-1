<p><p>The fire-fighting fabrics market encompasses various types of fabrics used in firefighting operations. Coated fabric refers to textiles coated with a layer of fire-resistant material for added protection. Aluminized fabric is made by depositing a layer of aluminum on the surface, reflecting heat and providing insulation. Fiberglass fabric is woven from glass fibers, offering excellent resistance to high temperatures. Non-woven fabric is produced by bonding fibers together without weaving, offering lightweight and breathable protection. These different fabric types cater to different firefighting needs and provide crucial protection against flames and heat.</p></p>
