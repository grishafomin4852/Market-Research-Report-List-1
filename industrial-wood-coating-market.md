<p><ul><li>Waterborne</li><li>Conventional Solvent Borne</li><li>High Solid Solvent Borne</li><li>Powder Coating</li><li>Radiation Cured</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The industrial wood coating market consists of various types, including Waterborne, Conventional Solvent Borne, High Solid Solvent Borne, Powder Coating, Radiation Cured, and Others. Waterborne coatings are water-based and environmentally friendly. Conventional Solvent Borne coatings use traditional solvents, while High Solid Solvent Borne coatings have a higher solid content, reducing VOC emissions. Powder coatings are applied as a dry powder and then cured with heat. Radiation Cured coatings use UV radiation to achieve a quick drying time. Other types refer to coatings that do not fit into these categories, such as oil-based coatings or specialty coatings.</p></p>
