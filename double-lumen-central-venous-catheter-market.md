<p><p>A double-lumen central venous catheter (CVC) is a medical device used for long-term central venous access. It consists of two separate channels or lumens within a single catheter, enabling simultaneous delivery of fluids, medication, and blood sampling. Double-lumen CVCs are typically used in critically ill patients who require multiple intravenous therapies or hemodialysis.</p><p>The future outlook for the double-lumen CVC market is promising. The increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, such as kidney failure and cancer, is driving the demand for long-term central venous access devices. Additionally, the rising geriatric population and advancements in healthcare infrastructure are further contributing to the growth of this market.</p><p>In terms of current outlook, the market for double-lumen CVCs is witnessing steady growth. The increasing adoption of these catheters in both hospitals and homecare settings is fueling market expansion. Moreover, technological advancements, such as the development of antimicrobial-coated catheters, are enhancing the safety and performance of double-lumen CVCs.</p><p>However, the market does face challenges.
