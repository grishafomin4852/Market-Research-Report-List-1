<p><p>COVID-19 diagnostic tests are medical tests used to detect the presence of the SARS-CoV-2 virus, which causes COVID-19, in an individual. These tests can be used to confirm infection in individuals with symptoms, screen asymptomatic individuals, and monitor the spread of the virus in communities. There are primarily two types of diagnostic tests available for COVID-19: molecular tests and antigen tests.</p><p>Molecular tests, such as polymerase chain reaction (PCR) tests, detect the virus's genetic material and are considered the gold standard for COVID-19 diagnosis. Antigen tests, on the other hand, detect specific proteins on the surface of the virus. Although less sensitive than molecular tests, antigen tests provide faster results and are more affordable, making them suitable for mass testing efforts.</p><p>The COVID-19 diagnostic tests market has witnessed significant growth since the outbreak of the pandemic. The market is driven by the increasing number of COVID-19 cases worldwide, the need for efficient testing to control the spread, and the development of innovative testing solutions.
