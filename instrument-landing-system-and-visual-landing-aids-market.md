<p><p>Instrument Landing System (ILS) is a ground-based radio navigation system that provides guidance to pilots for a precision approach and landing during adverse weather conditions, such as low visibility. It consists of two main components: a localizer, which guides the aircraft laterally, and a glideslope, which provides vertical guidance. ILS is commonly used in airports worldwide to ensure safe and accurate landings.</p><p>Visual Landing Aids (VLA) are a set of visual cues and markings on the runway and surrounding areas designed to assist pilots in visually aligning the aircraft with the runway during landing. These aids include runway markings, threshold markings, touchdown zone markings, runway lights, and approach lighting systems. VLAs are particularly useful during good weather conditions when pilots can rely on visual references to safely land the aircraft.</p></p>
