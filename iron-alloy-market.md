<p><p>Iron alloy is classified into three types based on the carbon content: high carbon, medium carbon, and low carbon. High carbon alloys have a carbon content ranging from 0.6% to 1.5%, making them hard and strong but less ductile. Medium carbon alloys contain 0.3% to 0.6% carbon and offer a balance between hardness and ductility. Low carbon alloys, with 0.02% to 0.3% carbon, are less brittle and easier to shape due to their increased ductility. These different market types cater to various industrial applications and consumer preferences.</p></p>
