<p><p>Pavement pervious material, also known as pervious concrete or porous asphalt, is a type of material used for constructing pavement surfaces that allows water to pass through it, reducing stormwater runoff and enabling water infiltration into the ground below. It consists of a combination of aggregate, cement, and water, with void spaces that enable water to move through the material.</p><p>The market analysis of the pavement pervious material industry reveals promising growth prospects. The growing concerns regarding stormwater management and environmental sustainability have been major drivers for the market. Pervious pavements offer numerous benefits such as reducing the risk of flooding, recharging groundwater, and removing pollutants, which have led to their increasing adoption in various applications including parking lots, sidewalks, driveways, and roads.</p><p>Additionally, governments worldwide have implemented regulations and incentives to promote the use of sustainable construction materials, further driving the demand for pavement pervious materials. These materials help reduce surface runoff and promote groundwater recharge, aiding in stormwater management and preventing flooding.</p></p>
