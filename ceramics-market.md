<p><p>Ceramics can be categorized into two main market types: traditional ceramics and advanced ceramics. Traditional ceramics refer to those that have been produced and used for centuries, such as pottery, porcelain, and earthenware. These ceramics are typically made from clay and are fired at high temperatures. On the other hand, advanced ceramics are more modern and technologically advanced materials, including ceramics used in electronics, aerospace, medical devices, and high-performance applications. They are often made using more complex processes and require precise engineering to achieve specific properties.</p></p>
