<p><p>The automotive aluminum wheel market can be categorized into three types: casting, forging, and others. Casting refers to the process of melting and pouring molten aluminum into a mold, allowing it to cool and solidify into the desired wheel shape. Forging involves shaping the wheel by applying heat and pressure to deform the aluminum into the desired shape. The "others" category includes various manufacturing techniques such as spinning, flow forming, and hybrid manufacturing that are used to create automotive aluminum wheels.</p></p>
