<p><p>Fluorescent materials are categorized into organic and inorganic materials in the market. Organic fluorescent materials are compounds with carbon-based structures that emit light when excited by energy sources like UV light. They are commonly used in applications like organic light-emitting diodes (OLEDs) and organic fluorophores for bioimaging. On the other hand, inorganic fluorescent materials are typically composed of minerals or metallic compounds that exhibit fluorescence. They find applications in areas such as display panels, security inks, and lighting solutions. Both organic and inorganic fluorescent materials contribute to various industries with their unique properties and applications.</p></p>
